Motivation and Introduction
Research Motivation
Most banks or lending institutions are updating their business models due to advanced
technologies related to big data, data availability and computing power.
The key to decision-making and transparency is credit risk prediction and model reliability.
To credit risk, in reality the datasets might be imbalanced and huge, this research will
also study ways to deal with these features.
3 / 31
Motivation and Introduction
Credit Risk Prediction and Model Reliability
The original paper studies 7 models: Elastic Net (EN), Random Forest (RF), Gradient
Boosting Machine (GBM) and four Deep Learning Models.
We extend the study by examining three more models related to GBM: Gradient Boosting
Decision Tree (GBDT), XGBoost and Light Gradient Boosting (lightGBM).
Deal with Imbalanced and Huge Datasets
Imbalanced: In original paper, the author used SMOTE algorithm, we extend the study by
comparing SMOTE with Random Over-sampling and Random Under-sampling.
Huge: The original paper studies whether use 10 features only with highest feature
importance can give better predictions.
Improved Methods
We propose two improved algorithms: Stacking and GBDT+LR .

More Advanced Algorithms
Detailed Stacking Ensemble Algorithm (with internal k-fold):
(1) Split the training data into K-folds just like K-fold cross-validation.
(2) Fit base models on the K-1 parts and predict for Kth part (reduce overtting).
(3) Do for each part of the training data.
(4) Fit base models on the whole training data and predict for the test set.
(5) Use the outputs as the festures for the second level model.
(6) Use level 2 model to make prediction on test set

More Advanced Algorithms
Choice of level 1 and level 2 models (for kaggle dataset, all with Random Oversampling):
Level 1: EN, GBM, GBDT (three best models for the dataset)
Level 2: Linear regression
By intuition, stacking would yield better results because it combines predictions from
multiple models and k-fold reduce overtting (no mathematical proof available).
However, the results is not that good as expected, we get:
Stacking RMSE(valid) AUC(valid) RMSE(test) AUC(test)
without k-fold 0.4754 0.6523 0.4881 0.6472
with k-fold 0.4013 0.6871 0.4381 0.6901
Possible reasons are:
(1) GBM and GBDT are related algorithms, not typically dierent.
(2) GBDT performs far better than the other two.
It also yield that if a base-model performs as well as or better than the stacking ensemble,
the base model should be used instead, given its lower complexity.
